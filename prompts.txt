TASK 1 — ETF MATH AUDIT (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Founder intuition: the math model may behave differently or 
incorrectly on ETF holdings vs. single stocks. No specific bug 
confirmed, but the suspicion is strong enough to warrant a 
systematic audit. Two of three /how-it-works sample portfolios 
are ETF-heavy — if math is wrong, published backtests are wrong.

Investigate across:
1. Return computation (lib/yahoo.ts TWR, FX-adjusted returns)
2. Distribution handling (dividends, ETF distributions)
3. Total-Return vs. Price-Return data fetching from Yahoo
4. Currency hedging detection and FX adjustment logic
5. Covariance estimation in Markowitz solver (lib/backtest)
6. Walk-forward backtest engine handling of mixed asset types
7. Sample-length handling when ETFs have shorter history

Test these 5 hypotheses:

H1: Yahoo Finance returns Price-Return data for some ETF tickers 
    and Total-Return for others, depending on suffix (.SW, .L, 
    .DE, .US). Code might treat them uniformly.

H2: ETF distributions (quarterly dividends) not incorporated into 
    return calculations, causing systematic under-reporting of 
    ETF returns vs. single stocks.

H3: Currency-hedged ETF share classes (e.g. AGGG.L is CHF-hedged) 
    being double-FX-adjusted because FX layer doesn't detect 
    that returns are already hedged.

H4: Markowitz solver behavior on ETF portfolios with high 
    inter-asset correlation (>0.9) produces unstable or 
    counterintuitive weights vs. single-stock portfolios.

H5: Walk-forward training-window length sometimes shorter for 
    ETF-heavy portfolios because some ETFs have less history, 
    leading to noisy covariance estimates.

Deliverable: /docs/ETF_MATH_AUDIT.md with:
- Section A — Code paths inspected (file paths, line numbers)
- Section B — Findings per hypothesis (confirmed | likely | 
  unlikely | refuted with evidence)
- Section C — Concrete Vitest test scenarios to add, using real 
  Yahoo data for VWRL.SW, CSPX.L, AGGG.L, EIMI.L vs. NESN.SW 
  and AAPL controls
- Section D — Severity assessment (does this affect /how-it-works 
  published backtests, paying user's metrics, FIDLEG risk)
- Section E — Fix proposal ranked by impact, effort, regression risk

Hard rules:
- Do NOT modify lib/yahoo.ts during audit
- Do NOT delete existing tests
- If audit finds NO bugs, say so clearly — don't manufacture issues
- If a hypothesis can't be tested without writing code, document 
  the test that would prove/disprove it
